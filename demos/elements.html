Elements

In our design system, it is classes – in the form of modifiers and widgets – that are primarily responsible for determining the presentation of things. Because there is a lot of variation in the presentation of HTML elements throughout our web GUI, our reset style sheet is aggressive. It removes many of the default styles applied to HTML elements by the web browser. This gives us a lower baseline design to build on. As a result, the rulesets for widgets and modifiers are leaner because they rarely need to unset inherited styles.

<a>

The anchor element, used to create hyperlinks, is one of the most frequently used HTML elements and its presentation varies significantly with context. Our reset style sheet removes most of the browser's default rulesets for this element.

<address>

The <address> element has been reclassified by the W3C as a sectioning element. For this reason we remove the default italicised text applied by most browsers. We also apply the clearfix hack to this and all other sectioning elements. Any nested floats are therefore automatically cleared by the <address> container.

<video>

The <video> element is converted from an inline to a block-level control, so this span of text should be rendered below the video not alongside it. Video containers are also made to never exceed the width of their container. However, while the video itself will scale proportionally, the height of the video box will remain consistent, resulting in a letterbox effect. So, we give video containers a black background so that the letterbox effect doesn't look so bad.
